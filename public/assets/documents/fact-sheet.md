# LIFTS Fact Sheet

## Organization Overview

**Name:** Launch Initiatives for Technologies in Space (LIFTS)  
**Type:** Student Aerospace Research Organization  
**Institution:** University of Puerto Rico at Mayagüez (UPRM)  
**Founded:** January 2024  
**Website:** https://liftspr.org  

---

## Mission Statement

To advance space technology and inspire the next generation of aerospace engineers through hands-on design, development, and launch of aerospace systems.

---

## Programs & Capabilities

### High-Altitude Balloon (HAB) Program - NEXO
- **Maximum Altitude:** 100,000+ feet (30+ km)
- **Payload Capacity:** Up to 6 lbs
- **Flight Duration:** 2-3 hours typical
- **Recovery:** GPS/APRS-tracked parachute descent; NEXO recovered intact on private property near Tyler, TX (~200 miles from launch)
- **Applications:** Atmospheric research, photography, sensor testing

### High-Altitude Balloon Program - ASCENT
- **Mission Type:** Puerto Rico-based high-altitude balloon mission
- **Target Altitude:** 100,000+ feet
- **Payload Envelope:** 2.5 kg across five scientific payload modules
- **Estimated Flight Time:** Approximately 3 hours
- **Status:** Upcoming mission planning

### CubeSat Program
- **Form Factor:** 1U CubeSat
- **Orbit Type:** Low Earth Orbit (approximately 400 km)
- **Subsystems:** Power, communications, flight software, structures, and payload
- **Target:** Future student-led UPRM satellite mission

---

## Technical Capabilities

- **Ground Station:** UHF/VHF tracking and telemetry
- **CAD/CAM:** SolidWorks, Fusion 360
- **Fabrication:** 3D printing, CNC machining
- **Electronics:** Custom PCB design and assembly
- **Software:** Flight computers, ground control systems

## Achievements

- Completed NEXO high-altitude balloon launch during the 2024 total solar eclipse
- 95,000+ feet maximum altitude reached by NEXO
- 100% recovery rate for completed LIFTS missions to date
- ASCENT mission planning for a 100,000+ foot Puerto Rico balloon flight
- Student training through payload development, tracking, recovery, and mission operations
